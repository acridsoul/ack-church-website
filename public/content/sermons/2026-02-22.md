## English Service Notes

**Preacher:** Vicar

**Bible Verses:** Isaiah 5:2-16, Luke 6:46-49

**Theme:** Building on the Right Foundation

### Sermon Notes

1. **God Has Invested in Us and Expects Fruit** - The vineyard owner in Isaiah dug the ground, removed the stones, planted the best vines and built a watchtower. God has lavished His care and blessing on us — He rightly expects a return.

2. **We Can Disappoint God** - Despite all the care given to the vineyard, it produced only wild and worthless grapes. When we live for ourselves and ignore God's ways, we grieve the heart of the One who loves us.

3. **Sin Has Consequences** - Isaiah lists the woes that come upon those who live wickedly — those who chase wealth, indulge in pleasure and call evil good. A life built apart from God ultimately collapses.

4. **Words Without Obedience Ring Hollow** - Jesus asks a pointed question: "Why do you call me Lord, Lord, and do not do what I say?" Many profess faith with their lips but their lives tell a different story.

5. **The Wise Builder Hears AND Obeys** - The person who hears Christ's words and puts them into practice is like a man who built his house on a rock. When the storms came, the house stood firm.

6. **The Foolish Builder Hears BUT Does Not Act** - Hearing alone is not enough. The man who heard but did not obey built on sand — and when the storm came, his house was completely destroyed.

7. **Lent is a Season to Examine Our Foundation** - As we begin this season of Lent, we are called to examine our lives honestly. What are we building on? Is our foundation the Word of God, lived out in daily obedience?

## Kikuyu Service Notes

**Mũrutani:** Vicar

**Gũthoma Ibuku:** Isaiah 5:2-16, Luke 6:46-49

**Theme:** Building on the Right Foundation

### Sermon Notes

1. **God Has Blessed Us Abundantly** - Like the vineyard owner, God has given us life, family, church and every good thing. He has removed obstacles from our path and surrounded us with His care.

2. **God Expects Obedience and Righteousness** - The vineyard was expected to produce good grapes. God expects the fruit of righteousness, justice and faithfulness from His people.

3. **There Are Consequences for Turning Away from God** - The woes of Isaiah warn us that those who live in pride, greed and wickedness face the judgement of God. Do not take His patience for granted.

4. **True Faith Leads to Action** - Jesus calls us to be doers of the Word, not hearers only. Attending church and calling on the Lord must be matched by a life of obedience.

5. **Build Your Life on the Rock of God's Word** - The storms of life — sickness, loss, hardship — will come for everyone. The only house that stands is the one built on obedience to Christ.

6. **Lent is a Time of Renewal and Return** - This Lenten season, let us turn back to God wholeheartedly, clear away anything in our lives that is not bearing fruit, and rebuild our lives on His sure foundation.
