## English Service Notes

**Preacher:** L/R Consolata

**Bible Verses:** Isaiah 6:1-10, Matthew 16:24-28

**Theme:** Called, Cleansed and Sent

### Sermon Notes

1. **God is Holy and Exalted** - Isaiah's vision reminds us that God sits enthroned on high, and all creation declares His glory. He is worthy of our full reverence and worship.

2. **We All Fall Short Before God** - When Isaiah encountered God's holiness, his first response was "I am ruined, for I am a man of unclean lips." Recognising our sinfulness is the beginning of a true encounter with God.

3. **God Cleanses Those He Calls** - A live coal from the altar touched Isaiah's lips and his guilt was taken away. God does not call the qualified — He qualifies the called.

4. **God is Still Asking — Who Will Go?** - "Whom shall I send? And who will go for us?" God's call is still open. Isaiah's answer was immediate: "Here am I. Send me." What is your answer?

5. **Discipleship Demands Self-Denial** - Jesus makes it clear: "Whoever wants to be my disciple must deny themselves, take up their cross and follow me daily." Following Christ is not comfortable — it is costly.

6. **Losing Your Life to Truly Find It** - The one who clings to their life will lose it, but the one who surrenders their life for Christ's sake will find true and abundant life.

7. **Nothing is Worth the Loss of Your Soul** - "What good will it be for someone to gain the whole world, yet forfeit their soul?" Keep an eternal perspective in all you do.

## Kikuyu Service Notes

**Mũrutani:** L/R Margaret

**Gũthoma Ibuku:** Isaiah 6:1-10, Matthew 16:24-28

**Theme:** Called, Cleansed and Sent

### Sermon Notes

1. **God is Holy and His Glory Fills the Earth** - Isaiah saw the Lord seated on a high and lofty throne. The seraphim declared "Holy, holy, holy is the Lord Almighty; the whole earth is full of his glory."

2. **No One is Worthy in Their Own Strength** - Like Isaiah, when we come before God we must acknowledge our sinfulness and unworthiness. Pride has no place before a holy God.

3. **God Purifies and Equips Us for His Service** - God sent a seraph with a burning coal to purify Isaiah. He removes our guilt and shame so that we can serve Him faithfully.

4. **Answer God's Call Without Delay** - God is calling each one of us. Do not delay or make excuses. Say as Isaiah said — "Here am I, send me."

5. **The Way of the Cross is the Way of Life** - Jesus says we must take up our cross daily. There is no discipleship without sacrifice and commitment.

6. **Put God Above All Earthly Things** - No wealth, position or comfort in this world is worth losing your place with God. Seek first the Kingdom of God.
